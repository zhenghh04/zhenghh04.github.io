About

Academic Biography

I am a Computer Scientist in the Data Science group at Argonne National Laboratory (ALCF). My research interests span data management and parallel I/O, large-scale distributed training, and agentic AI for autonomous scientific discovery. I apply high-performance computing and deep learning to domain sciences including physics, chemistry, materials science, astrophysics, and earth systems. I also co-lead the MLPerf Storage Benchmarking group.

I received my Ph.D. in Physics from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign in 2016, with a background in condensed matter physics, including first-principles simulations using density functional theory and Quantum Monte Carlo. I joined ALCF as a Theta Early Science postdoc in 2016 and transitioned to the Data Science group in 2018.

My current projects include: (1) ExaIO/ExaHDF5 — advanced HDF5 for efficient parallel I/O; (2) data management and I/O optimization for AI workloads; (3) scalable image reconstruction; and (4) agentic AI systems for orchestrating autonomous scientific workflows across DOE facilities.

Personal

I grew up in a small village in southern China. Both of my parents are farmers who instilled in me the value of education. My curiosity about nature led me to pursue physics through a Ph.D., while deeper questions about the meaning of life eventually led me to faith. I became a Christian in Hong Kong in 2010 and have found that my work, family life, and personal life are all means to grow and find purpose.

I am married and live in Glen Ellyn, IL with my wife and two children. Outside of work, I enjoy hiking, walking in nature preserves, and distance running — I have completed seven full marathons.
